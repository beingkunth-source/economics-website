Macroeconomics

Macroeconomics looks at the economy as a whole – total output, overall prices, jobs, money and trade with the rest of the world.

Key Questions in Macroeconomics

Output and Growth

How much does an economy produce in a year? How fast is this output growing over time? Gross Domestic Product (GDP) is the standard measure.

Inflation

How quickly are prices rising on average? Moderate inflation is normal, but very high or very low inflation can create problems.

Unemployment

What share of people who want to work cannot find jobs? Unemployment shows whether an economy is using its labour resources fully.

Policy Tools

Macroeconomic policy uses two main sets of tools:

  • Fiscal policy – taxes and government spending decided by the government.
  • Monetary policy – control of money supply and interest rates, usually carried out by the central bank.
